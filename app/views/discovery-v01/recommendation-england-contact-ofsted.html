Contact Ofsted

You should contact Ofsted about your concerns.

Ofsted will review your complaint and may discuss it with the school at the next scheduled inspection.

Ofsted must refer your complaint to other appropriate organisations if it raises concerns about the safety or wellbeing of a child.

State schools only

Ofsted can only accept complaints about state schools, academies and free schools (including residential schools).

What Ofsted cannot help with

Ofsted do not resolve disputes between parents and schools. They may keep your complaint on file for their next inspection, but don't always provide a response to those submitting a complaint.

Ofsted cannot:

  • resolve disagreements between you and a school
  • affect the results of a school's complaints process
  • investigate private schools, sixth form or further education colleges
  • consider complaints about admissions, exclusions, special education needs, religious education or the religious character of a school
