Raise your concern with the school

Contact the school to discuss the problem first - most problems can be solved this way.

Raise a concern

You can raise your concern with the school by making an informal complaint.

Speak to a teacher or the school's leadership.

The school will tell you when they will respond.

After they review your complaint, you'll be told the outcome.

When to talk to a teacher

Talk to the child's teacher if your concern is about something that happened in a lesson your child was in.

It is best to talk to the teacher about things like homework, pupil friendships and behaviour.

When to talk to middle or senior school leadership

Speak to the school's middle or senior leadership, like a departmental head, if the teacher is unable to resolve the problem, or the concern is about more than just one lesson.

You should speak to middle or senior leadership about bullying, additional support for your child or repeated behaviour issues.

When to talk to the headteacher or principal

Discuss your concern with the headteacher or principal if it is about a whole-school issue or if talking to teachers and middle leadership has not resolved the problem.

Make a formal complaint

If the problem is not resolved, follow the school's complaints procedure to make a formal complaint.

Every school in England must have a complaints procedure.

It's usually available on the school's website or from the school office and should tell you the kind of complaints the school deals with.
