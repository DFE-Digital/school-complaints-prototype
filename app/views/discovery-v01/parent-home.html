Complain about a school in England

If you are unable to resolve your complaint by talking to the school, it may be eligible for referral to another organisation in the education system for further review.

This may include your local authority, the Department for Education, Ofsted, or the Teaching Regulation Authority.

If you continue, you will be asked to complete a form capturing the details of your complaint.

Speak to the school first

Your complaint cannot be considered unless you have completed the school's complaint process.

As a first step, discuss your concerns with the school. This is often the quickest and simplest way to resolve an issue.

Unless someone is in danger, you must try to resolve your complaint with the school before any other organisation can get involved.

Contact the police if anyone is in danger

Call the police if a child or adult is in danger, or you believe a crime has been committed.

Call 999 in an emergency, or call Crimestoppers on 0800 555 111 to report something anonymously.

What happens next

After completing this form, your complaint will be reviewed to understand whether it should be escalated beyond the school.

We will get in touch to share the outcome of this review, and advise on next steps.

Note that in most cases another organisation cannot overturn decisions made by a school's governing body, force the school to apologise, or discipline any pupils or staff.

If your complaint is eligible for referral, the details you've submitted will be shared with the relevant organisation(s).

Before you begin

You will need to provide evidence relating to your complaint. This may include:

  • Information about the nature of the complaint, and why you would like to escalate it.
  • Copies of any correspondence, such as emails or letters.
  • Evidence that you have completed the school's local process, such as the dates of meetings and decisions.

You cannot submit video recordings as evidence.
